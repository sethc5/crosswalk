## Exemption Category Claimed

**45 CFR 46.104(d)(2)**: Research that only includes interactions involving educational tests (cognitive, diagnostic, aptitude, achievement), survey procedures, interview procedures, or **observation of public behavior** (including visual or auditory recording) if at least one of the following criteria is met:

**(i)** The information obtained is recorded by the investigator in such a manner that the identity of the human subjects **cannot readily be ascertained**, directly or through identifiers linked to the subjects.

## Justification for Exemption

### Nature of the Research

This study evaluates the effect of an infrastructure modification (overhead crosswalk illumination) on driver and pedestrian behavior at public roadway crossings. The study:

1. **Observes public behavior in public spaces**: All data collection occurs on public roadways and sidewalks. No private spaces are observed.

2. **Does not intervene on human subjects**: The intervention is on the infrastructure (LED fixture installation), not on any person. Drivers and pedestrians are not research participants — they are observed engaging in their normal behavior on public roads.

3. **Does not collect personally identifiable information**:
   - Radar data: Records vehicle speed and pedestrian presence. Does not identify individual vehicles or persons.
   - Activation logs: Record system events (timestamp, duration, trigger source). No human identifiers.
   - Video data (for yielding rate coding): Records public roadway activity. No names, license plates (not recorded at resolution sufficient to read), or other identifiers are collected. Video is used for aggregate behavioral coding (yield/no yield, crossing path) and is not published with identifiable individuals.

4. **Cannot reasonably cause harm**: Observation of public behavior on roadways cannot place subjects at risk of criminal or civil liability, financial damage, or social harm. The infrastructure modification (crosswalk lighting) is a safety improvement — it cannot reasonably harm any observed individual.

### Regarding Video Recording

Video recording of public roadways for traffic analysis is standard practice in transportation research and is routinely classified as exempt by university IRBs. The video data in this study:

- Is recorded at standard traffic-camera resolution
- Is used solely for aggregate behavioral coding
- Is retained for the duration of the study analysis period
- Will not be published or shared in any form that would allow identification of individuals
- Will be destroyed after coding is complete and verified

### If Linked to Physiological Data

**NOTE**: If this study is linked to an existing wearable-sensor pedestrian safety study (e.g., smartwatch heart rate variability data), the physiological data component IS human subjects research that requires informed consent and operates under a separate IRB protocol. Linking the two datasets would require:

- Amendment to the existing IRB protocol for the physiological study
- Consent from physiological study participants to link their data with infrastructure performance data
- De-identification of the linked dataset prior to analysis

The infrastructure observation component of THIS study remains exempt regardless of whether it is linked to a physiological study.

## Study Population

No enrolled participants. The study observes:
- Drivers operating vehicles on public roadways (estimated hundreds per day per crossing)
- Pedestrians using public crosswalks (estimated 20-100 per day per crossing)

No recruitment, no consent, no interaction with observed individuals.

## Data Security

- Radar and activation data stored on encrypted controller, transmitted via encrypted cellular/LoRa backhaul
- Video data stored on encrypted portable hard drives during study period
- All data stored on institutional secure servers during analysis
- No cloud storage of video data
- Video destroyed after coding verification
- Aggregate (non-identifiable) data retained permanently for publication and CMF Clearinghouse submission

## Risk Assessment

**Risk to subjects**: Minimal. No greater than risks encountered in normal daily life (crossing a public street, driving on a public road). The infrastructure modification (crosswalk illumination) is a safety improvement that reduces risk to pedestrians.

**Risk of identification**: Negligible. No personally identifiable information is collected. Video recording is at standard traffic-camera resolution and is coded for aggregate behavior only.

## Requested Determination

Exempt under 45 CFR 46.104(d)(2)(i): Observation of public behavior recorded without identifiers.
