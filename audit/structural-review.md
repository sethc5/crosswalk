# Structural Review Checklist

Items requiring review by a structural engineer or PE before pole selection and installation.

## Pole Design

- [ ] **Wind load**: ASCE 7-22 basic wind speed for installation site. Nashville = 115 mph (Risk Category II). Calculate wind pressure on pole, fixture, solar panel, and mounting arm per ASCE 7-22 Chapter 29.
- [ ] **Ice load**: ASCE 7-22 ice loading. Nashville is in a moderate ice zone (~0.5-0.75 inch radial ice thickness). Calculate added weight and wind-on-ice load for solar panel and fixture.
- [ ] **Combined loading**: Dead load (fixture + panel + battery enclosure + hardware) + wind + ice. Verify pole capacity for combined loading.
- [ ] **Pole material and section**: Steel, aluminum, or fiberglass? Round tapered, square, or octagonal? Wall thickness and section modulus adequate for combined loading?
- [ ] **Effective projected area (EPA)**: Solar panel is the largest wind sail. Calculate EPA for panel + fixture + arm + pole at the design wind speed. Consider panel orientation (flush-mount vs tilted).

## AASHTO LTS-6 Compliance

- [ ] **Fatigue design**: AASHTO LTS-6 fatigue category for the pole and arm connection type. Vibration from wind and traffic. Arm connection detail must meet fatigue requirements.
- [ ] **Deflection**: Maximum deflection at fixture height under design wind. Excessive deflection affects aim accuracy.

## MASH Compliance

- [ ] **Clear zone**: Is the pole within the AASHTO Roadside Design Guide clear zone for the road's speed and volume? On curbed urban roads, minimum 1.5 ft lateral offset from face of curb.
- [ ] **Breakaway base**: If within clear zone, pole base must be MASH-compliant (breakaway or yielding). Verify FHWA eligibility letter exists for the selected pole/base combination.
- [ ] **Federal aid**: If federal funds (HSIP, SS4A) are used AND the road is on the NHS, MASH compliance is mandatory, not optional.

## Foundation

- [ ] **IBC 2021 Section 1807.3**: Foundation design for embedded pole or concrete footing. Lateral bearing capacity of soil at installation site.
- [ ] **Depth**: Embedment depth per IBC Table 1807.3.2.1 or engineering calculation for the site's soil conditions.
- [ ] **Frost line**: Nashville frost depth is approximately 12-18 inches. Foundation bottom must be below frost line.
- [ ] **Existing utilities**: Call 811 before excavation. Verify no conflicts with underground utilities at pole location.

## Retrofit-Specific (Mounting on Existing RRFB Pole)

- [ ] **Existing pole capacity**: What is the original pole rated for? Adding fixture (10-30 lb) + solar panel (20-40 lb) + battery enclosure (50-100 lb) + mounting arm — does the existing pole have reserve capacity?
- [ ] **Existing foundation capacity**: Was the original foundation designed with reserve for additional loads?
- [ ] **Connection detail**: Arm-to-pole connection method. Banding, bolted, welded? Fatigue implications.
- [ ] **If pole is inadequate**: New independent pole on approach side. Adds cost but eliminates structural uncertainty. May be the safer default for a spec that targets many different existing installations.

## Solar Panel Mounting

- [ ] **UL 2703**: Mounting system listed to UL 2703 for structural, grounding, and bonding.
- [ ] **Wind uplift**: Panel tilt angle (30-35° for Nashville) creates uplift under wind. Verify mounting hardware resists uplift at design wind speed.
- [ ] **Snow/ice shedding**: Tilted panel will shed ice/snow — verify no pedestrian hazard below.
